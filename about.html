The Personal Journey Behind This Project

If you’re reading this — thank you. For real. Maybe I asked you to check it out. Or maybe you landed here on your own, curious. Either way, I’m glad you're here. And if you read it all the way through? I hope you let me know.

Like any human being, I craved love. I longed for connection. I wanted that partner-in-crime type of love. The problem was, I attached quickly. Fell fast. And while friends shared stories of long-distance trust or live-in heartbreak, I was navigating a reality where I gave trust freely — and got betrayed twice.

My partner had an avoidant attachment style. Always distant. Always on guard. And still, I stayed. I forgave her. I believed “everyone makes mistakes.” At the time, I thought I was securely attached. Turns out, that experience flipped something inside me. I became anxious. Overthinking. Second-guessing. Needing more than I ever used to.

That’s when I started searching for answers. I dove into the 5 Love Languages. Then came the book Attached by Amir Levine and Rachel Heller. That book didn’t just help me understand her — it helped me understand me. Why I react. Why I over-care. Why I cling, why I withdraw.

Oh, and — I have ADHD. That adds another layer. Rejection Sensitivity Dysphoria (RSD) is real. It’s why even the smallest emotional distance can feel like abandonment. Why I read between the lines even when there’s nothing written there. ADHD didn’t just shape my brain. It shaped my attachment too.

This project was born out of all of that. Out of hurt. Growth. Reflection. Learning. I didn’t build this quiz for clicks or clout. I built it for people like me. For the version of me who once thought, “Why can’t I get the love I deserve?”

In today’s world — fast, swipable, impatient — love feels like war. You're not just looking for connection anymore. You're fighting for someone’s attention. But real love? Real connection? That comes when you pause to understand. Yourself. Your patterns. Your partner.

“In a world where separation often feels easier than connection, learning how we attach helps us see that love isn’t about finding someone new — it’s about showing up, understanding, and growing with the one we’re with.”
