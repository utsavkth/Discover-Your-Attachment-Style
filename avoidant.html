Avoidant Attachment

Home Theory Quiz

You Value Independence — Sometimes at a Cost

Avoidant attachers often protect themselves by keeping emotional distance. Love is real — but so is the fear of being overwhelmed or losing autonomy.

Deactivating Behaviors

  • 🔻Focusing on flaws in your partner
  • 🔻Pulling away when things feel “too close”
  • 🔻Delaying commitment or avoiding labels
  • 🔻Craving space — but not explaining why
  • 🔻Fantasizing about being single again

What You Might Say

“I just need time to think. I don’t want to talk about it right now.”

Attachment Matchups: How It Feels

  • 💬 Avoidant + Avoidant: Minimal emotional demands may feel peaceful, but deep intimacy can stagnate or be avoided entirely.
  • 💬 Avoidant + Anxious: One chases, the other runs. Emotional needs clash, often creating conflict, misreading, and exhaustion.
  • 💬 Avoidant + Secure: A chance to grow — if the avoidant accepts emotional feedback and the secure stays grounded in self-worth.

Avoidance isn’t cruelty — it’s protection. But long-term connection requires letting safe people get a little closer.
